About

When I was little, I wanted to be the next Bill Gates. I liked that he never gave up even when he was about to lose a lot of profit from Microsoft but he still went to the court and won against IBM.

I spent my childhood designing websites, managing forums - moderating, working with PHP & MySQL, fixing computers - for schools, family and friends.

Since then a lot of things have changed.

After I graduated from primary school, my aptitude gradually leaned towards software development. I wanted to be a part of the programmers' society and build robust, cutting-edge programs that will benefit others.

My decision to go into a Computer Science degree was not a surprise for my career guidance counsellor.

Somehow, I managed to finish my first year of college despite my continuous financial and personal problems. It was the most valuable year of my life. I dropped out of college, but I showed extreme persistence in achieving my goals.

Persistence paid off!

I worked in a computer repair shop for three years. I routinely mentored work experience students, managed the entire shop and displayed a great deal of leadership and interpersonal skills. The three years of managerial work led me to discover and improve a lot of my qualities.

Today, I am back in college, studying Computing with Software Development.
